Our Group

The Quantum Fluids of Light group studies systems in which light and matter interact so strongly that they give rise to new collective quantum states known as polaritons.

The physics of polaritons is an interdisciplinary field that brings together concepts and techniques from multiple areas, including:

  • Materials physics and chemistry
  • Optics and photonics
  • Electronics
  • Nanostructure fabrication and characterization
  • Quantum many-body theory

Through the joint work of theoretical and experimental researchers, we explore fundamental phenomena and test theoretical models in cutting-edge experiments.

Research

Our experimental research is carried out across three laboratories at the Institute of Physics:

  • the Low-Dimensional Semiconductors Laboratory,
  • the Advanced Nanophotonics Laboratory, and
  • the Magnetization Dynamics Laboratory.

Students

The group maintains open opportunities for students at different levels—undergraduate research, bachelor’s theses, master’s, and PhD—who are interested in exploring matter and light from a quantum perspective.
